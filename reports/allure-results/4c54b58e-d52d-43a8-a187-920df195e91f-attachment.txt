Step-by-Step Guide: Renewing Your UAE Visa (2025)

Renewing your UAE residence visa is essential to maintain your legal status and access to services in the country. The process is streamlined and can be completed online or in person. Below is a clear, step-by-step guide based on the latest official procedures and expert recommendations.

1. When to Start
Begin your renewal process up to 30 days before your current visa expires.
There is a 30-day grace period after expiry, but renewing before expiry avoids fines and complications. 1 3
2. Choose Your Renewal Channel

You can renew your visa through any of the following official channels:

Online:
GDRFA Dubai website: 4
ICP (Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs, and Port Security) portal
Smart Apps: GDRFADUBAI, DUBAINOW
In Person:
Amer Centers (government-approved service centers)
Typing centers authorized by the government

1

3. Prepare Required Documents

The documents may vary by visa type, but generally include:

Original passport (valid for at least 6 months)
Current Emirates ID (and renewal application)
Passport-sized photos (as per UAE specifications)
Medical fitness test results (for applicants 18+)
Valid health insurance (as required by emirate)
Tenancy contract or proof of accommodation (for family/dependent visas)
Additional documents for dependents (e.g., study certificate for male children over 18, marriage certificate for daughters over 18)

3

4. Complete Medical Fitness Test
Schedule and attend a medical fitness test at an authorized center.
Required for applicants 18 years and older.
Results must be submitted with your renewal application.

1

5. Renew Emirates ID
Submit an application for Emirates ID renewal simultaneously with your visa renewal.
This is now a mandatory, linked process.

1

6. Submit Application & Pay Fees
Upload or submit all required documents via your chosen channel.
Pay the applicable fees (which may include medical, insurance, typing, and visa stamping fees).
Fees vary by visa type and emirate.

2

7. Wait for Processing
Processing time is typically 3–10 business days, depending on the emirate and completeness of your documents.
Dubai often has faster processing due to its digital infrastructure.

2

8. Receive Updated Visa & Emirates ID
Once approved, your new visa will be stamped in your passport.
Your renewed Emirates ID will be issued.
Important Tips
Double-check all documents for accuracy and validity.
Attend your medical test at an authorized center.
Update your personal details if there are any changes.
For family/dependent visas, ensure the sponsor meets salary and accommodation requirements.
Golden Visa and Green Visa holders may have extended grace periods and simplified renewal steps.

1

Where to Get Help
For personalized assistance, you may visit an Amer Center or consult with authorized visa service providers.
Always use official government portals for online applications to avoid scams.
